## Getting Started

### Logging In

1. Navigate to the **Teacher Login** page
2. Enter your **username** (provided by administrator)
3. Enter your **password**
4. Click **Login**

> **Note:** If you don't have login credentials, contact your school administrator.

### First Time Setup

On your first login:
- Verify your profile information
- Check your assigned subjects and classes
- Familiarize yourself with the dashboard

## Entering Grades

### Accessing Grade Entry

1. Navigate to **Results** in the navigation menu
2. Select the **Academic Year** and **Term**
3. Choose the **Class/Program**
4. Select the **Subject** you're teaching
5. Click **Enter Grades** or **Add Grades**

### Entering Student Grades

For each student, enter:

1. **Class Mark** - Continuous assessment marks (usually out of 40 or 50)
2. **Exam Mark** - Examination marks (usually out of 60 or 50)
3. **Total** - Automatically calculated (Class Mark + Exam Mark)

âœ… **Complete Entry** - Ensure all students have grades entered

### Submitting Grades

1. After entering all grades, click **Submit** or **Save**
2. Grades will be marked as **Pending** for approval
3. Wait for admin approval before results are published

> **Note:** Once submitted, you may need admin approval to make changes.

## Approving Results

### Understanding the Approval Process

Results go through this workflow:
1. **Pending** - Grades entered, awaiting approval
2. **Accepted** - Approved and published
3. **Rejected** - Returned for correction

### Approving Results

If you have approval permissions:

1. Go to **Results** page
2. Find results with **Pending** status
3. Review the grades
4. Click **Approve** to publish or **Reject** to return for correction

### Rejecting Results

If results need correction:
1. Click **Reject** on the result
2. Add a **Reason** or **Comment** (if required)
3. The result will be returned to the teacher for correction

## Teacher Remarks

### Adding Remarks (Class Teachers Only)

If you're assigned as a **Class Teacher**, you can add remarks:

1. Navigate to **Remarks & Reports**
2. Select the **Academic Year** and **Term**
3. Choose the **Class**
4. For each student, add:
   - **General Remarks** - Overall performance comments
   - **Strengths** - What the student does well
   - **Areas for Improvement** - Areas needing attention
   - **Recommendations** - Suggestions for parents/guardians

### Remark Options

You can use pre-configured remark options or write custom remarks:
- Select from **Remark Options** dropdown
- Or type **Custom Remarks**

### Printing Remark Slips

1. After adding remarks, click **Generate Remark Slip**
2. Review the slip
3. Click **Print** to print for distribution

## Viewing Results

### Viewing Class Results

1. Go to **Results** page
2. Select **Academic Year** and **Term**
3. Choose **Class/Program**
4. View all students' results in that class

### Viewing Subject Results

1. Navigate to **Results**
2. Select **Academic Year** and **Term**
3. Choose **Class/Program**
4. Select **Subject**
5. View all students' performance in that subject

### Result Statistics

You can see:
- **Class Average** - Average marks for the class
- **Highest Score** - Top performing student
- **Lowest Score** - Student needing support
- **Pass Rate** - Percentage of students who passed

## Troubleshooting

### Can't Enter Grades

**Problem:** Grade entry page not accessible or not loading

**Solution:**
- Verify you're assigned to that subject
- Check if the academic year/term is correct
- Contact administrator if subject assignment is missing

### Grades Not Saving

**Problem:** Grades disappear after entering

**Solution:**
- Check your internet connection
- Ensure all required fields are filled
- Try refreshing and re-entering
- Contact support if issue persists

### Can't Approve Results

**Problem:** Approval button not available

**Solution:**
- Verify you have approval permissions
- Check if results are in "Pending" status
- Contact administrator about permissions

### Remarks Not Showing

**Problem:** Remarks & Reports menu not visible

**Solution:**
- Verify you're assigned as a class teacher
- Check with administrator about class teacher assignment
- Only class teachers can add remarks
